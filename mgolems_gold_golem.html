Behavior

Gold Golems are village defenders. They wander around their village, chase down hostile mobs and hit hard: 24 attack damage per swing, immune to knockback. They ignore Creepers and only turn on players who attack them first.

During the day, if any hostile mob comes within 20 blocks, the Gold Golem winds up a Solar Flare. After a short beacon-activate charge it releases a flash that applies Slowness III for 5 seconds to every enemy in range and paints them with lingering wax particles. Cooldown is 30 seconds. At night the ability does not trigger.

Spawning

Gold Golems appear in two ways: they show up wherever a regular Iron Golem would spawn in a Jungle biome, and they can also be built by hand anywhere by stacking a Carved Pumpkin on top of an iron-golem-shaped body of Gold Blocks.

Natural Spawning

When a village or any structure attempts to spawn an Iron Golem inside the Jungle biome, More Golems replaces it with a Gold Golem. Jungle villages defend themselves with gilded guardians instead of the usual iron ones.

Building

Build the same T-shape you would use for a vanilla Iron Golem, but out of Gold Blocks: two blocks stacked for the body and legs, one block on either side of the upper body for the arms, then a Carved Pumpkin (or Jack o'Lantern) on top. The blocks are consumed and the golem spawns in place.

  • Carved Pumpkin or Jack o'Lantern (1)
  • Gold Block (4: body, legs, two arms)

Interactions

Gold Ingot (right-click): heals the golem for 25 and consumes one ingot, up to full health.

Drops

On death the golem drops 3 to 5 Gold Ingots.

  • Gold Ingot (3–5)
